### **Suggestion to Include Additional Information**

When redesigning the substance information component, consider including:

- **Chemical Structure Diagram (ASCII Art):** A simplified representation.
- **Legal Status:** Information about legality in different regions.
- **Medical Uses:** Approved medical applications and therapeutic benefits.
- **Common Sources and Forms:** Natural sources and available formulations.
- **Metabolism and Half-life:** How the body processes the substance.
- **Subjective Effects:** Personal experiences and common reports.
- **Safety Tips and Contraindications:** Recommendations for harm reduction.
- **Tolerance and Dependency Information:** How tolerance develops and resets.
- **Interactions with Other Substances:** Both synergistic and antagonistic.
- **Warnings and Precautions:** Important safety information.

These designs aim to present substance information in a more engaging and informative way, enriching the content with
additional details that users might find valuable.

**Key elements to include in your redesign:**

- **Clear Organization:** Separate sections for different types of information.
- **Visual Cues:** Use icons and symbols to represent different data points.
- **Additional Data:** Legal status, metabolism, safety tips, interactions, and more.
- **User Engagement:** Interactive elements if possible, even within a CLI context.
- **Readability:** Ensure that text-based graphics are clean and not overly cluttered.
- **Comprehensive Information:** Include missing parts that users may find beneficial.

Feel free to mix and match elements from these examples to suit your application's needs!
